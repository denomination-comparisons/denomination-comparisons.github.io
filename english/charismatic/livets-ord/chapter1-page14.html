When God speaks to mankind, He does so with a purpose. He wants to reveal Himself, His power and His will to man, and to grant him knowledge, salvation and fellowship. God does not reveal Himself to man merely to provide general information that satisfies his curiosity. He reveals Himself in order to restore man to Himself.

Because he is estranged from God, revelation is essential to man. His sins separate him from God (Isaiah 59:2) and he cannot bridge the gap on his own. God Himself must save him, which is why He reveals Himself. Before the Fall, God spoke openly to man, but since then, He has only spoken to him through revelation.

General Revelation

Revelation can be divided into two categories: general and specific revelation.

General revelation is primarily God's revelation of Himself in creation, in nature. In Romans, we read:

...because what may be known of God is manifest in them (mankind), for God has shown it to them. For since the creation of the world His invisible attributes are clearly seen, being understood by the things that are made, even His eternal power and Godhead, so that they are without excuse... (Romans 1:19-20).

God created the world so that all mankind can understand that He exists. Creation testifies unceasingly to its Creator. That is why the testimony of Psalm 53:1 is true: The fool has said in his heart, "There is no God." Only fools suppress the truth (Romans 1:18) by denying what nature is unfailingly telling them: "There is a God."
