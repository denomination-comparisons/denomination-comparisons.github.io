Revelation [Page 13] (Right-hand page)

Christianity is a "revelation-religion." When Peter said to Jesus, "You are the Christ," Jesus responded, Blessed are you, Simon Bar-Jonah, for flesh and blood has not revealed this to you, but My Father who is in heaven (Matthew 16:16-17). Only through revelation could Peter and the other disciples realize that Jesus was the Messiah, foretold by the prophets.

Jesus Himself came to earth by revelation and as a revelation. In his first epistle, John says: That which was from the beginning, which we have heard, which we have seen with our eyes, which we have looked upon, and our hands have handled, concerning the Word of Lifeâ€”the life was manifested, (i.e. was revealed) and we have seen... (1 John 1:1-2)

God's revelation of Himself, His will, His counsel and purposes came to us straight from His heart. Through the Holy Spirit, this was imparted to the prophets who then wrote it down in the Bible. Hebrews 1:1 says, God, who at various times and in various ways spoke in time past to the fathers by the prophets, has in these last days spoken to us by His Son...

What then, is revelation? Revelation can be defined as a supernatural act of God that reveals truth to man. Paul describes it like this. But as it is written: "Eye has not seen, nor ear heard, nor have entered into the heart of man the things which God has prepared for those who love Him.
