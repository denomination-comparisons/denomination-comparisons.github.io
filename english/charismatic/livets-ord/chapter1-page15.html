God has written His law in man's conscience where it produces an intuitive sense of what is right and what is wrong. The conscience can, of course, be numbed, hardened, deadened and even seared, but nonetheless it always testifies to the existence of God.

SPECIFIC REVELATION

While creation and conscience together declare that God exists, this knowledge is not sufficient to save man and grant him fellowship with God. That is the purpose of specific revelation. Through specific revelation God enables man to perceive who he really is, to recognize his true plight and his need of God and salvation.

Such revelation can come in many ways. For example, God speaks through dreams, visions, angelic visitations, direct speech or through signs and wonders. God has spoken at many times and in various ways (Hebrews 1:1).

When God revealed Himself to Abram for the first time (Genesis 12:1-3), it was so that all mankind should be blessed. Abram kept all that God commanded him and passed his knowledge on to his son, Isaac, who then handed it down to Jacob.
